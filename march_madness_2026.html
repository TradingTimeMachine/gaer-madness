▮ What Each Metric Means
KenPom Net Rating DEFAULT 45%
The gold standard — adjusted offensive minus defensive efficiency per 100 possessions. Historically the single best predictor of tournament success. Most serious models weight this 40-55%.
NET Ranking DEFAULT 20%
The NCAA's own metric — what the selection committee actually uses. Correlates strongly with seeding. Overlaps with KenPom so don't double-weight it too heavily.
Quad-1 Win % DEFAULT 15%
How a team performs against the best opponents (away vs top 30, neutral vs top 50). Teams that win big games in the regular season tend to win big games in March. Underweighted by most public models.
Strength of Schedule DEFAULT 10%
How hard a team's schedule was. Important context for efficiency metrics — a 25-5 record in a weak conference is very different from 25-5 in the Big Ten. Mid-majors often get penalized here.
Seed Adjustment DEFAULT 10%
The committee's seeding encodes information the metrics miss — injuries, recent form, travel, geography. Gives a small bump to higher seeds as a prior. Reduce this if you trust the analytics over the committee.
